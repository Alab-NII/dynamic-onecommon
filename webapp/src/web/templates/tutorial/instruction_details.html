Tutorial

Instruction Details

  • When you chat with your partner, please use natural language as much as possible.
    • Describe your view and ask about your partner's view to verify you're talking about the same dot.
    • You should use various spatial expressions (above, between, along, ...) and temporal expressions (after, before, while, ...).
    • Remember that you have slightly different views! You should describe the positions/movements of the dots in relation to other (nearby) dots and make sure they match.
    • Do not rely solely on color and size. Use placements and movements effectively.
    • Avoid complex numbers, metrics, symbols and abbreviations: 11 o'clock, 30 degrees, 2 inches, /, \, ~, %, etc.
    • Avoid talking about anything irrelevant with solving this game (including questions about how to use the interface).
  • Please make it very sure you found the same dot before you select! You need to succeed at least 2 turns to submit your HIT.
  • Please be aware that your work will not be accepted if these instructions are not followed carefully.

Effective Strategies

To find the same dots effectively, you can discuss the ...
  • Present State (Views at the end of each turn)
    • "Now, the black dot is to the left and slightly above a lighter dot."
    • "One light dot stopped almost right between 2 darker dots."
  • Dynamic Change (How your view changed)
    • "After moving together to the right, the two dots spread apart."
    • "There is a small black dot following the lighter dot."
  • Previous States (Views in the previous turns)
    • "In the previous turn, it was very close to a black dot."
    • "Before they moved, the lighter dot was far above the darker dot."
